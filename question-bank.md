# Question bank — 80 starters

The heart of the whole thing. Every question here is: about something ordinary, hiding a non-obvious answer, specific, findable from a single decent page, and never spoiling itself. Drop these straight into the `Queue` to fill the first couple of months, and let the generator extend them in the same spirit once you run low.

Eight domains, ten each. Mix freely — a good day's menu of three pulls from different domains so the flavors don't collide.

---

## Kitchen & food
- Why do recipes tell you to preheat the oven instead of just turning it on when the food goes in?
- Why does bread go stale faster in the fridge than on the counter?
- Why does cutting an onion make you cry?
- Why does a cut apple turn brown, and why does lemon juice stop it?
- Why does honey never spoil?
- Why does reheated rice sometimes make people sick when other leftovers don't?
- Why do bananas make the fruit next to them ripen faster?
- Why does chocolate sometimes develop a white, powdery film?
- Why does pasta water boil over so suddenly right when you look away?
- Why do hard-boiled eggs sometimes get a green-grey ring around the yolk?

## The body
- Why do we get goosebumps, including from music?
- Why does your stomach growl, and why mostly when it's empty?
- Why is yawning contagious?
- Why do you get a "stitch" in your side when you run?
- Why do your fingers go pruney in water but the rest of your skin doesn't?
- Why does time seem to speed up as you get older?
- Why do some songs get stuck in your head on a loop?
- Why does scratching an itch feel good?
- Why does your nose run in cold weather?
- Why do we hiccup, and why is it so hard to stop?

## Household objects & built environment
- Why do elevators have mirrors?
- Why are stop signs octagons specifically?
- Why do public restroom stall doors have a gap at the bottom?
- Why are most manhole covers round?
- Why is the "close door" button in many elevators not actually wired to anything?
- Why does the keyboard use QWERTY instead of alphabetical order?
- Why are there small bumps on the F and J keys?
- Why do brick walls have little gaps or holes near the bottom?
- Why is there a tiny hole in airplane windows?
- Why do clocks in adverts almost always show 10:10?

## Social customs & language
- Why do we say "bless you" when someone sneezes?
- Why do we clink glasses when we toast?
- Why do we shake hands, and why specifically the right hand?
- Why does English have so many silent letters?
- Why is "OK" understood in almost every language on earth?
- Why do we put candles on a birthday cake and blow them out?
- Why do phone keypads run 1-2-3 along the top but calculators run 7-8-9?
- Why do we knock on wood for luck?
- Why is junk email called "spam"?
- Why are American and British spellings different (color vs colour)?

## Money & everyday systems
- Why do so many prices end in .99?
- How does a self-checkout know you didn't scan something?
- How does a newspaper set its crossword and sudoku difficulty across the week?
- How does a vending machine tell a real coin from a slug?
- How does a barcode actually work — what do the lines mean?
- Why do some supermarket trolleys need a coin to unlock?
- How does contactless payment work without the card touching anything?
- How do they choose what becomes "hold music"?
- Why do credit cards still have raised, embossed numbers?
- Why does the stock exchange open and close with a bell?

## Nature, weather & everyday science
- Why is the sky blue during the day but red at sunset?
- Why can you see your breath when it's cold?
- Why does the air smell a particular way just after rain?
- Why does the moon look huge near the horizon but small overhead?
- Why does ice float when almost every other solid sinks?
- Why does thunder rumble on instead of making a single bang?
- Why do mirrors seem to flip left-right but not up-down?
- Why don't birds get electrocuted sitting on power lines?
- Why does static electricity shock you more in winter?
- Why do leaves change color before they fall?

## Everyday tech
- How does a thermos keep hot things hot and cold things cold in the same flask?
- How do noise-cancelling headphones actually cancel noise?
- How does a touchscreen know exactly where your finger is?
- How does a key fob unlock your car from across the parking lot?
- Why does "turn it off and on again" fix so many things?
- How does GPS on your phone know where you are?
- Why do phone batteries noticeably worsen after a year or two?
- How does a QR code store a whole web address in a little square?
- How does a microwave heat the food but sometimes not the plate?
- Why does Wi-Fi slow down the more devices are connected?

## Games & puzzles
- How does a newspaper generate a sudoku that has exactly one solution?
- Why does a standard deck have 52 cards?
- How are dice made to roll fairly?
- Why is a chessboard 8×8 and not some other size?
- Why are there jokers in a card deck, and what were they originally for?
- How does a pinball machine decide your score?
- Why do darts boards have that particular jumbled number order?
- Why does Monopoly money come in those specific denominations?
- How does a Rubik's cube have a "solved" state you can always reach?
- Why are billiard balls numbered and colored the way they are?

---

## Notes for wiring this in
- Paste into the `Queue` tab with `status = queued`. Add an `est_minutes` guess per row (most of these are 15–30; a few — GPS, noise-cancelling, sudoku generation — run to 60).
- Feed this whole list to the generator as its "recent questions" seed too, so its first inventions don't accidentally repeat what's already here.
- These set the bar. When the generator drifts toward the obvious ("why is the sky blue" is borderline — most people half-know it), that's your signal to tighten the prompt.
